Why San Diego?

America's 'Finest City', on the Californian Pacific Coast

San Diego (Spanish for 'Saint Didacus') is a city located in the extreme south-west of the U.S. state of California. With a population of over 1.3 million residents, the city is the eighth-most populous in the United States and the second-most populous in California after Los Angeles. San Diego is known for its mild year-round Mediterranean climate, extensive beaches and parks, its long association with the United States Navy, and its recent emergence as a healthcare and biotechnology development centre. The city's airport, San Diego International Airport, is the busiest single-runway airport in the world.
